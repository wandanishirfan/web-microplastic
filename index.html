Detailed Exposure Pathways

Food chain: Bioaccumulation occurs as microplastics move up the food chain, with highest levels in top predators.

Drinking water: Both tap and bottled water contain microplastics, with bottled water having higher concentrations.

Air: Indoor air typically contains more microplastics than outdoor air, mainly from textiles, building materials, and household items.

Personal care: Products applied to skin may contain microbeads or be packaged in plastics that leach compounds.
